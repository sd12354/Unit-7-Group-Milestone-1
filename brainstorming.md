# Activity 1: App Idea Brainstorming

## Step 1 — Brainstorming ideas (6+)

1. **StudySync** — Students post study sessions (time, place, subject); others browse, join, and get reminders. Optional: simple group chat per session.
2. **ShelfLife** — Personal reading tracker: books, progress, quotes, yearly goals; optional friends feed for recommendations.
3. **SplitBite** — Roommates share a grocery list in real time, assign who buys what, and log shared expenses with simple totals.
4. **PlantParent** — Watering and care reminders per plant, photo growth timeline, and care tips based on plant type.
5. **CampusPulse** — Clubs and orgs post events; students filter by category, RSVP, and add events to calendar.
6. **FocusFlow** — Pomodoro timer tied to a class/task list, weekly stats, and optional “study with me” live status with friends.
7. **LocalLoop** — Hyper-local bulletin: free stuff, rides to airport, sublets near campus; report/flag for safety.

---

## Step 2 — Evaluate top 3 ideas

We scored each idea on: **Mobile**, **Story**, **Market**, **Habit**, **Scope**.

### Idea A — StudySync

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Mobile** | Strong. Push notifications for session reminders, maps/deep links to meeting spots, camera optional for whiteboard notes. Less compelling as a desktop-only product. |
| **Story** | Clear narrative: “Find your people and actually study together.” Reduces friction from group chats and scattered DMs. |
| **Market** | Primary: college and high-school students, bootcamp cohorts. Secondary: coworking / certification study groups. Large, reachable audience on campus. |
| **Habit** | Medium–high. Spikes during midterms/finals; steady if users run recurring weekly sessions. Not necessarily daily for everyone. |
| **Scope** | Medium. MVP: auth, create/browse/join sessions, profile. Later: chat, recurring sessions, school-specific feeds. |

### Idea B — ShelfLife

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Mobile** | Good for quick logging and scanning book covers; reading is often on paper or another device, so the phone is a natural “sidecar.” |
| **Story** | “Your reading life in one place.” Appealing to book lovers; weaker if the group prefers more social / real-time apps. |
| **Market** | Readers, students, book clubs. Smaller than generic social, but passionate niche. |
| **Habit** | Occasional. Users open when starting/finishing a book—needs strong reminders or social hooks to bring them back. |
| **Scope** | Narrow to medium. MVP is manageable (lists, progress); social layer adds complexity. |

### Idea C — SplitBite

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Mobile** | Very strong. Grocery runs happen on the go; push when list updates; camera for receipts. |
| **Story** | Practical: “Stop arguing about who owes what.” Clear value in shared housing. |
| **Market** | Roommates, families, trip groups. Competitive space (Splitwise, etc.)—differentiation must be tight (e.g., list + split in one flow). |
| **Habit** | High during semester; weekly or more for active households. |
| **Scope** | Medium–high. Real-time sync and expense logic are trickier for a first capstone than a mostly CRUD + events model. |

---

## Final idea (chosen)

**StudySync** — We chose this because it has a **strong mobile story** (notifications + location), a **clear user problem** (organizing study meetups), a **defined student market**, **reasonable scope** for an MVP with room to grow, and **natural screens** (feed, detail, create, profile) that map well to tabs and navigation stacks for the course.
